Combat Information

Jumping

While unconventional, jumping is a viable form of combat that can be capitalized in several different applications, perhaps even giving an upper hand to those that will need it. When making the Attack action, you can use your attack to make a Jump attack against an enemy, so long as you have five feet of movement available to spend for the initial jump. When using the Jump attack, make an attack roll using Strength or Dexterity to deal damage based on your size category:

  • Tiny: 1d4
  • Small: 1d6
  • Medium: 1d8
  • Large: 1d10
  • Huge: 1d12
  • Gargantuan: 2d8

Danger and Peril

As combat pushes you to your absolute limits, you will find yourself in situation where you are at risk of falling at any given moment. This is known as being in Danger, and it acts as both a warning for your overall status and a means of pulling yourself back from the brink. You gain the Danger Status when you are at 30% HP and below, and gain the Peril Status when you are at 10% HP and below, both of which are rounded down.


Guarding and Super-Guarding

When stuck in the thick of combat, taking damage will be an inevitability. Those aware of that, however, make due by ensuring that they can mitigate that damage by any means, or better, unleashing a counter attack to inflict damage onto their enemy.


Guarding: When taking damage from any source of damage, you can expend one of your hit die to reduce the damage taken. The damage reduced is the roll of the hit die + your Constitution modifier. Additive bonuses to AC, such as equipped shields and the Shield Spell are added to the damage reduced when guarding.


Super-Guarding: When hit by an attack roll, you can use your reaction to attempt to intercept the attack by making a contested attack roll with a melee weapon of your choosing. If your roll exceeds the attack roll made against you, you take no damage. If the attack roll was a melee attack, you automatically deal damage to the attacking creature equal to the damage roll your weapon uses. NOTE: Super-Guarding WILL NOT work against Critical Hits. You can, however, crit a Super-Guard.

Stylish Attacks

Sometimes you attack with such expert precision that you make the attack Stylish. If you hit an attack roll where the result exceeds the target's AC by 5 or more, the amount of SP you gain during your turn is doubled. Critical Hits will always count as Stylish, regardless of whether you confirm it or not.
